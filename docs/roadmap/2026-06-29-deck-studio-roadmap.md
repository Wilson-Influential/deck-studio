# Deck Studio Roadmap

Updated: 2026-06-29  
Source: Viv and Connor meeting notes

## Product Aim

Deck Studio should become a brand-safe internal deck builder for non-designers. The product test is whether a regular team member can produce a credible Influential presentation quickly without breaking layout, colour, image, or typography rules.

## Current Baseline

- Brand-locked deck creation is working.
- Export paths include PDF, PNG, share link, and PowerPoint.
- Custom image upload and movement are available.
- Undo and redo are in progress.
- Alex is the main real-world tester.

## Priority 1: Template Expansion

Build the next layout set around common agency deck needs:

- Graph and chart pages.
- Costings and budget pages.
- Project timing and roadmap pages.
- Excel-style data tables.
- Pie chart and bar chart pages.
- Copy-heavy explanatory pages.
- Case study pages.
- Team pages with three to six circular headshots.

The team page is a particularly strong "Jane proof" feature because it removes manual image cropping and prevents poor source image handling.

## Priority 2: Asset Library

Improve the library from a basic asset list into a searchable brand picker:

- Searchable imagery.
- Searchable custom icons.
- Headshot library.
- Brand scribbles and hand-drawn accents.
- Stronger labels and metadata for functional searches, such as "magnifying glass".

Asset dependency: Viv and Connor are gathering the source material from SharePoint.

## Priority 3: Creative Handoff

PowerPoint export is already useful for people who want to tweak advanced layouts manually.

Explore additional export paths for creative users:

- Figma export.
- Adobe Illustrator export.
- Possibly Canva handoff for social or lightweight editing.

These should support manual polish after the tool generates a strong brand-safe starting point.

## Priority 4: Testing Rollout

Move testing beyond Alex once the next template batch exists:

- Chris Humes.
- Karen.
- Duncan.
- Other frequent deck builders.

Ask testers to bring real presentation needs, not artificial test cases. The tool should be judged by whether it helps them make the decks they already need to make.

## Later: Multi-Client Support

Keep Deck Studio focused on the Influential internal brand until the experience is stable.

Only then explore client brand swapping. Multi-client support should be treated as a later product layer, not a reason to complicate the internal version now.

## Adjacent Roadmap: Social Post Studio

The Social Post Studio idea should remain separate from Deck Studio.

Potential direction:

- Visualizer or inspiration generator, rather than a rigid template builder.
- Safe-zone checkers.
- Aspect ratio toggles.
- Instagram-friendly output.
- Fast ideation workflow, followed by export to Figma, Illustrator, or Canva for manual refinement.

Deck Studio should stay structured and presentation-led. Post Studio can be looser and more exploratory.
